Fixing scientific notation

One rule. Two directions. Master this and Q7 and Q10 become easy.

After a multiplication or division, the answer sometimes lands outside proper scientific notation. You must normalise it — adjust the coefficient until it sits between 1 and 10, then fix the exponent to match.

The normalising rule:
— Coefficient ≥ 10 (too big)  →  move decimal left, increase the exponent
— Coefficient < 1 (too small)  →  move decimal right, decrease the exponent

Example 1  — Multiplication (result too big)

Problem (5 × 10²) × (4 × 10³)
→ Multiply coefficients:  5 × 4 = 20
→ Add exponents:  10² × 10³ = 10⁵
→ Raw answer:  20 × 10⁵  — 20 is ≥ 10, not allowed
→ Move decimal left:  20 → 2.0, exponent increases:  10⁵ → 10⁶
✓ Answer:  2 × 10⁶

Example 2  — Division (result too small)

Problem (2 × 10⁵) ÷ (4 × 10²)
→ Divide coefficients:  2 ÷ 4 = 0.5
→ Subtract exponents:  10⁵ ÷ 10² = 10³
→ Raw answer:  0.5 × 10³  — 0.5 is < 1, not allowed
→ Move decimal right:  0.5 → 5.0, exponent decreases:  10³ → 10²
✓ Answer:  5 × 10²
